Retail Sales Analysis & Dashboard (2022)
* Overview *

In this project, I worked on a retail sales dataset from 2022 with around 31,000 records. The dataset included details like customer demographics, order information, sales channels, product categories, and sales amount. The goal was to understand how customers are buying and what factors are driving overall sales.

Instead of just analyzing the data in tables, I focused on building an interactive dashboard in Excel so that insights can be explored more easily and visually.

* Objective *

The main aim was to answer some practical business questions, such as:

Which customers contribute the most to sales?
When do sales peak during the year?
Which states and platforms generate the most revenue?

These insights can help in making better decisions around marketing and targeting.

* Data Cleaning & Preparation *

Before building the dashboard, I cleaned and prepared the data to make sure the analysis was accurate. This involved removing duplicate entries, handling missing values, and making category names consistent.

I also created age group buckets to better understand different customer segments instead of working with raw age values.

* Dashboard Development *

Once the data was ready, I built an interactive dashboard using Excel. I used Pivot Tables for analysis and charts for visualization, along with slicers to make the dashboard dynamic.

The idea was to keep it simple and easy to use, so that anyone can quickly filter the data and understand trends without going into the raw dataset.

* Key Insights *

From the analysis, a few clear patterns stood out:

Women accounted for around 69% of total purchases
Sales were highest in March, showing a noticeable peak
Maharashtra generated the highest revenue among all states
Online platforms like Amazon contributed the largest share of sales

These insights help in identifying which customers and regions are most important for the business.

* Impact *

This dashboard makes it easier to quickly understand sales trends and customer behavior without going through thousands of rows of data. It can be useful for identifying target customers, tracking performance, and making more informed business decisions.
